Overview
Time
4 months
Software
Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe InDesign, Microsoft Sway
Roles
Art director, Graphic Designer
Context
The PACWEST Volleyball Championships is a volleyball event involving multiple post-secondary institutions in British Columbia. This year (2024), Douglas College hosted the championship, automatically entering both the women's and men's volleyball teams into the event. I designed and developed a visual identity for the event's print and digital materials, including posters, score tempaltes, and ID badges.
Deliverables
Sports brackets (digital and physical), Physical Poster, ID badges
Sports Brackets

Physical Sports Bracket

A sports bracket was required for both 6 ft x 3ft and 4 ft x 2ft prints. A sports bracket is a visual representation of the winners of each match. Brackets show who won between two teams, which leads to the next match between those winners. The blank squares were filled during the event with stickers of the logos.

Physical Sports Bracket in person

Mobile Program Guide Bracket

I adapted the bracket for a vertical mobile device. The challenge was to still keep that familiarity, while making it more accessible for vertical devices, such as phones.


Physical Poster

Final Draft, in person

Physical posters were designed to be put up around campus, promoting the event and showcasing some of the athletes participating. The athletes were in a way to create a slight curve towards the right. This was to give the column of athletes more visual interest, and allow plenty of space for the athletes holding volleyballs.

First Draft

The first draft was my initial take of the event: professional and clean, and dramatic. The contrast of the text and background colours weren't as strong, and the proportions of the athletes in relation to each other weren't fully developed yet.

Second Draft

The second draft involved a change of the top right athlete due to team roster changes. I also changed all the text and icons to white to be cohesive with the PACWEST championships logo.

Final Draft

After feedback, the poster became more casual with a change to a simple line of text on the bottom bar, and the relocation and resizing of the date and location. The background was also changed to green to align with the athletic programâ€™s branding colours.

ID Badges

Front

Back

Double sided ID badges were designed to be worn by staff, athletes (left), and VIP members. The challenge was to fit lots of information on the back, while staying brief yet understandable in the dates and times. I used hierarchy and blocking of information to achieve this.

Reflection

Use imagery. Show athletes, logos, patterns, use as much as necessary, because people want to see that over lots of text. Instagram was a great indicator of this; there was more engagement and comments on the event announcement post with the poster because athletes were included in the graphic.

I also expanded my knowledge in designing smaller merch, such as the ID badges. The badges were approximately 4 x 6 inches, and I often overestimated how large I needed to make the text. I wasn't confident that the schedule information was readable, as it was very condensed into a small section. However, when seeing the physical copies of the badges, I was pleasantly surprised to see how readable the text was. Now, I have a clear understanding of how using measuring tools in real life can help greatly with determining how graphics will look on paper.

I learned that creating graphics for a large events to be perceived by many requires a recognizable and consistent visual branding, involving colours, typography, and layout of elements. It helps the people attending the event to be able to automatically group pieces of media together with similar visual branding.
